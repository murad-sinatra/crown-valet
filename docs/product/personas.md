# Personas

## Customer

### Profile

A guest, resident, patient, diner, traveler, or event attendee who uses valet parking and wants a smooth experience without waiting or uncertainty.

### Goals

- Drop off the car quickly.
- Know the car is safe and traceable.
- Request pickup before reaching the valet stand.
- Add useful services while the car is parked.
- Pay and tip without friction.

### Pain Points

- Paper tickets are easy to lose.
- Pickup wait times are unclear.
- The customer does not know whether a requested service was completed.
- Damage claims are stressful without proof.
- Payment and tipping can feel rushed at the curb.

### Key Features

- Digital valet ticket
- Vehicle status timeline
- Pickup request
- Service add-ons
- Digital payment and receipt
- Push, SMS, and email notifications
- Support and incident reporting

## Valet Attendant

### Profile

A front-line team member who receives vehicles, captures condition details, creates tickets, and communicates with customers.

### Goals

- Check in vehicles quickly.
- Capture accurate customer, vehicle, and key details.
- Avoid mistakes during rush periods.
- Communicate status clearly to runners and managers.

### Pain Points

- Manual forms slow down the line.
- Handwritten tickets can be illegible or incomplete.
- Vehicle damage may be blamed on valet staff without evidence.
- Customer requests can be missed during busy shifts.

### Key Features

- Fast vehicle check-in
- QR code ticket creation
- License plate and vehicle profile capture
- Condition photos
- Key tag assignment
- Customer notification triggers

## Valet Runner

### Profile

A valet team member responsible for parking and retrieving vehicles.

### Goals

- Know which car to move next.
- Find the car quickly.
- Update vehicle status without extra paperwork.
- Reduce pickup delays.

### Pain Points

- Parking locations are communicated verbally or by memory.
- Cars can be blocked in or hard to find.
- Pickup priority is unclear.
- Managers may not know which runner has which vehicle.

### Key Features

- Parking location assignment
- Pickup queue
- Runner assignment
- Vehicle status updates
- Internal notes
- Delay alerts

## Valet Manager

### Profile

The person responsible for shift performance, staffing, issue resolution, and venue service quality.

### Goals

- Monitor active vehicles and staff workload.
- Keep pickup wait times low.
- Resolve issues before they affect guests.
- Track performance by shift, staff member, and venue.

### Pain Points

- Lack of real-time visibility into active tickets.
- Hard to prove what happened during disputes.
- Staffing decisions are based on guesswork.
- Add-on service progress may be outside the valet team's normal workflow.

### Key Features

- Operations dashboard
- Staff activity feed
- Wait time monitoring
- Incident management
- Service status tracking
- Shift reporting

## Venue Owner or Operator

### Profile

A hotel, restaurant, event, residential, medical, airport, or retail operator that uses valet as part of the guest experience.

### Goals

- Improve guest satisfaction.
- Reduce curb congestion and complaints.
- Create premium service revenue.
- Understand valet performance across days, events, and locations.

### Pain Points

- Guest complaints can damage the venue brand.
- Valet service quality is difficult to measure.
- Partner services are disconnected from parking operations.
- Revenue opportunities are missed when services are not promoted digitally.

### Key Features

- Venue dashboard
- Branded customer experience
- Revenue reporting
- Service catalog configuration
- Customer satisfaction tracking
- Multi-location analytics

## Platform Administrator

### Profile

An internal Crown Valet operator or system administrator responsible for onboarding venues, configuring products, and supporting accounts.

### Goals

- Configure new venues quickly.
- Manage roles, pricing, services, and integrations.
- Support refunds, disputes, and account issues.
- Maintain auditability and compliance.

### Pain Points

- Manual venue setup does not scale.
- Role permissions can become risky without structure.
- Support teams need a full history of customer, valet, and payment events.
- Pricing and service rules vary by venue.

### Key Features

- Venue setup
- Role-based access control
- Pricing and service configuration
- Audit logs
- Refund and support tools
- Reporting exports

## Service Partner

### Profile

A business or team that fulfills add-on services such as cleaning, detailing, fuel delivery, EV charging, or maintenance checks.

### Goals

- Receive complete service instructions.
- Know the vehicle location and timing constraints.
- Update status for customers and valet staff.
- Provide proof of completion.

### Pain Points

- Requests may arrive through phone calls or texts without structured details.
- Vehicles can be moved before service is complete.
- Payment and revenue share may be difficult to reconcile.
- Customers may dispute service quality without proof.

### Key Features

- Partner task queue
- Service request details
- Completion photos
- Status updates
- Timing windows
- Revenue reconciliation

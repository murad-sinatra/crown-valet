# Market and Revenue

## Market Opportunity

Valet parking is often treated as a basic convenience, but for many venues it is one of the first and last touchpoints in the guest experience. A slow drop-off, confusing pickup, or unresolved vehicle issue can affect the customer's perception of the entire visit.

Crown Valet positions valet parking as a premium digital service layer that can improve satisfaction, increase operational visibility, and create new revenue from vehicle services.

## Target Venue Segments

### Hotels and Resorts

- High guest expectations and strong need for branded service.
- Opportunities for overnight charging, detailing, luggage coordination, and VIP handling.
- Multi-day guest relationships create repeat service opportunities.

### Restaurants and Nightlife

- High peak demand over short windows.
- Pickup wait time has strong impact on customer satisfaction.
- Tips, premium pickup, and quick cleaning services can add revenue.

### Event Venues and Stadiums

- Large traffic spikes before and after events.
- Need for queue control, staffing visibility, and guest communication.
- Event-based pricing and pre-booking can improve throughput.

### Luxury Residential Buildings

- Recurring users and known vehicles.
- Strong fit for subscriptions, resident profiles, monthly billing, and scheduled services.
- Building management benefits from reporting and issue tracking.

### Hospitals and Medical Centers

- Customers may be stressed, delayed, or mobility constrained.
- Pickup coordination and clear communication are especially valuable.
- Accessibility, reliability, and support workflows are important.

### Airports and Private Terminals

- Higher-value parking and vehicle services.
- Strong fit for fuel, EV charging, detailing, and scheduled pickup.
- Travelers value status visibility and receipts.

## Stakeholder Value

### Venue Partners

- Better guest experience with digital ticketing and proactive updates.
- Lower curbside confusion during busy periods.
- New revenue from services, premium options, and tips.
- Reporting that supports staffing, vendor management, and service quality.

### Valet Operators

- More efficient staff coordination.
- Fewer paper-based errors.
- Better proof for disputes and claims.
- Performance data by shift, role, and location.

### Service Partners

- Structured demand from vehicles already parked at partner venues.
- Clear job instructions and proof requirements.
- Easier payout and revenue reconciliation.

### Customers

- More confidence after handing over keys.
- Less waiting and less uncertainty.
- Convenient vehicle care while already parked.

## Revenue Streams

### Software Subscription

Venues or valet operators pay a monthly platform fee based on location count, volume, features, or staff seats.

Possible tiers:

- Starter: digital ticketing, pickup requests, basic dashboard.
- Professional: service add-ons, payments, notifications, reports, role management.
- Enterprise: multi-location analytics, custom integrations, advanced permissions, dedicated support.

### Transaction Fees

Crown Valet can earn a percentage or flat fee on processed transactions:

- Valet fees
- Tips
- Add-on vehicle services
- Premium pickup or reservation options
- Corporate or event parking packages

### Service Revenue Share

For partner-delivered services, Crown Valet can receive a marketplace commission or revenue share.

Examples:

- Car wash
- Interior cleaning
- Detailing
- Gas fill-up
- EV charging
- Tire pressure or light maintenance checks

### Venue Revenue Share

Venues can share revenue from service add-ons and premium options. The model should be configurable by venue contract.

### Corporate and Residential Accounts

Recurring users can be billed through:

- Monthly resident plans
- Corporate parking accounts
- Hotel guest folios
- Event packages
- Membership programs

### Data and Reporting Packages

Advanced reporting may be packaged for larger operators that want insights across multiple locations.

## Pricing Considerations

Pricing should reflect volume, operational complexity, and revenue opportunity.

Key variables:

- Number of venues
- Monthly valet session volume
- Number of staff users
- Payment processing volume
- Service marketplace volume
- Support level
- Integration needs
- Branding and customization needs

## Go-To-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Pilot Venue

Launch with one high-touch venue where valet operations are visible and guest experience matters. Focus on digital tickets, pickup requests, status updates, and basic reporting.

### Phase 2: Service Add-Ons

Introduce vehicle services once the valet workflow is reliable. Start with services that are easy to fulfill and verify, such as exterior wash or interior cleaning.

### Phase 3: Multi-Venue Expansion

Add venue dashboards, configuration, and reporting that support additional locations.

### Phase 4: Partner Marketplace

Build repeatable partner workflows and revenue share models for fuel, EV charging, detailing, and maintenance services.

## Business Metrics

- Monthly active venues
- Valet sessions per venue
- Customer digital ticket adoption
- Pickup requests per session
- Add-on service conversion rate
- Average revenue per session
- Gross merchandise value
- Partner service completion rate
- Refund and dispute rate
- Venue retention
- Net revenue retention

## Risks and Mitigations

- Operational adoption risk: keep staff workflows simple and train by role.
- Service quality risk: require proof of completion and partner SLAs.
- Customer privacy concern: show useful status while limiting exact location exposure.
- Payment complexity: use established payment providers and clear receipt rules.
- Peak-time reliability: design pickup queue and status updates for high-volume periods first.
